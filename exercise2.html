Diaphragmatic Breathing

Diaphragmatic breathing, also known as belly breathing or abdominal breathing, is a technique that engages your diaphragm for deeper, more efficient breathing. Unlike shallow chest breathing, this method maximizes oxygen intake and promotes relaxation.

Instructions:

  1. Lie on your back with your knees bent, or sit comfortably in a chair.
  2. Place one hand on your chest and the other on your belly.
  3. Breathe in slowly through your nose, letting your belly rise while keeping your chest relatively still.
  4. Exhale slowly through pursed lips, feeling your belly lower.
  5. Repeat for 5‚Äì10 minutes, focusing on the rise and fall of your abdomen.

Tips:

  • Practice on an empty stomach for greater comfort.
  • Start with 5 minutes and gradually increase the duration.
  • Use the hand on your belly to ensure it's moving more than your chest.

Cautions:

  • If you feel dizzy, slow down your breathing or stop.
  • Consult a healthcare provider if you have respiratory conditions.
